Cape Cod Baseball League

Baseball Game at Veteran's Field

The Cape Cod Baseball League was founded in 1885 and is the premiere amateur summer baseball league in the United States. Each year, hundreds of college baseball players hope to get selected to play for one of the 10 teams in the league. The league consits of two divisions, East and West, with five teams playing within the two divisions. The Cape Cod Baseball League is run solely by volunteers and depends on donations from fans and sponsorships from small businesses and the MLB.

All-Star Game 2019 at Eldredge Park

Every year, the Cape Cod Baseball League has an impressive number of former players drafted into the major leagues. The 2019 draft included 14 former Cape Cod league alums in the first round. As of 2018, there were over 300 MLB players who had spent at least one summer playing for the Cape Cod Baseball League durin their college years. Year after year, the Cape Cod League is proudly represented in the major leages as so many of them spent time on the Cape.

Each season starts in the beginning of June and lasts until the Championship series in August. Games take place almost every day between all 10 teams. The teams are based in Wareham, Bourne, Falmouth, Coutuit, Hyannis, Yarmouth-Dennis, Harwich, Brewster, Chatham and Orleans. Admission to the games are free, encouraging as many people as possible and attract hundreds of tourists each year.
